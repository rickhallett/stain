# Source: https://sive.rs/ff | Author: Derek Sivers | Date: 2010

A leader must have courage to stand alone and appear foolish, but make their actions simple and easy to follow. The first follower plays a crucial role by publicly demonstrating how to participate, transforming a lone nut into a leader.

Being a first follower requires bravery — you risk standing out and facing mockery. The second follower represents a turning point, creating critical mass where three is a crowd and a crowd is news. Movements require visibility; new participants emulate other followers rather than the original leader.

As momentum builds, joining becomes increasingly low-risk. People who hesitated before now participate to avoid being excluded from the growing crowd.

The core insight challenges conventional wisdom: Leadership is over-glorified. While the initial figure receives credit, the first follower's contribution proved transformative. Movements cannot exist without followers willing to courageously stand up and demonstrate participation.

Rather than everyone pursuing leadership roles — which proves ineffective — the path forward involves finding inspiring individuals and having the guts to be the first person to stand up and join in, thereby inspiring others to follow.

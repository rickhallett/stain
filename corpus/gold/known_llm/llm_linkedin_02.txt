We need to talk about AI in education.

Not the hype. Not the fear. The reality.

I've spent the last three months working with schools implementing AI tools, and what I've seen has fundamentally changed how I think about the future of learning. It's not about replacing teachers — it's about amplifying what great teachers already do.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: most AI-in-education conversations are happening in boardrooms, not classrooms. Decision-makers are asking "how do we integrate AI?" when they should be asking "what problems do our teachers actually face?"

The difference is subtle but profound.

When we start with the teacher's experience, we find that AI excels at three things: personalising feedback at scale, reducing administrative burden, and identifying students who are struggling before they fall through the cracks.

But here's what most people get wrong — they assume technology solves problems by itself. It doesn't. Technology amplifies the system it enters. If your school culture is broken, AI will accelerate that brokenness. If your teachers are empowered, AI will multiply their impact.

To be fair, there are legitimate concerns. Privacy, equity, and the risk of over-reliance on automated assessment are real and deserve thoughtful engagement. And yet, the cost of inaction is equally real.

The schools that will thrive aren't the ones with the biggest tech budgets. They're the ones with the clearest vision, the strongest teacher support, and the courage to experiment.

This isn't about AI. It's about learning. And that's what makes this so important.

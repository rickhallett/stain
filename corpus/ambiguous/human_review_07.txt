I bought this keyboard six months ago expecting to hate it and send it back. I've typed roughly 300,000 words on it since then so clearly I was wrong.

It's a mechanical board with Cherry MX Brown switches, which the internet will tell you are the worst switch and everyone who uses them is a coward who should commit to either linear or clicky. I find them perfectly fine. The tactile bump is there. It's not dramatic. Kind of like me.

Build quality is solid. No flex. The keycaps are PBT which means they won't get shiny and disgusting after six months like the ABS caps on my last board. The cable is USB-C and detachable. There's no RGB because it's not a toy and I'm not streaming to an audience of twelve-year-olds.

Two complaints. The spacebar has a slightly higher pitched sound than the other keys and once you notice it you can't unnotice it. I've put a small piece of foam inside which helped about 60%. Second, the feet could be grippier. On my desk it slides about a millimeter when I'm typing hard, which happens when I'm angry and writing emails I shouldn't send.

Would I buy it again? Yes. Would I recommend it? Depends on whether you want a keyboard or a personality. It's a good keyboard. It's not going to change your life. If your typing sucks, it'll suck on this keyboard too. Just with a nicer sound.

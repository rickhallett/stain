I moved to Berlin in 2012 because a girl I was dating said it was cheap. She was right about that and wrong about most other things, including the relationship, which ended four months later in a Kreuzberg Kneipe over an argument about whose turn it was to buy toilet paper.

Berlin in 2012 was still in that phase where everyone was pretending they'd just discovered it. Americans who'd read one article in the New York Times. British people fleeing Tory austerity. Australians on working holiday visas doing exactly what they did in London but with cheaper beer.

I stayed seven years. I learned enough German to order food and argue with Vodafone customer service. I never learned enough to be funny, which in German is reportedly possible though I remain skeptical.

The city changed around me in a way that was imperceptible month to month but obvious year to year. The club next to my flat became a juice bar. The juice bar became a coworking space. The coworking space is now probably a luxury apartment. I don't know. I stopped checking.

When people ask me why I left I usually say rent, which is partly true. The real answer is more boring: I was tired. Not of Berlin specifically but of being an expat, which is a lifestyle that's exciting at 26 and exhausting at 33. You can only reinvent yourself in a new city so many times before you want to just be yourself in a place where you know how the bins work.

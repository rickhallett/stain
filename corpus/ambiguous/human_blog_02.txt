The thing about running a small agency is nobody tells you how much time you'll spend on things that have nothing to do with the work. Invoicing. Chasing invoices. Apologizing for chasing invoices. Updating your portfolio with work from two years ago because you haven't had time to photograph the recent stuff. Answering emails from people who found you on Google and want a "quick chat" that turns into an hour of free consulting.

Last month I tracked where my time actually went. Design work: maybe 35%. The rest was admin, emails, a surprising amount of Figma plugin troubleshooting, and one afternoon I lost entirely to setting up a new printer that still doesn't work.

My friend who works at a big agency thinks I'm living the dream. I think she's living the dream. We're probably both wrong.

The actual design work, when I get to do it, is still the best part. There's a moment when a layout clicks and you feel it before you can explain it. That hasn't gone away in twelve years. Everything around it has gotten harder and more tedious but that core thing — moving shapes and type around until something happens — that still works on me.

I should probably hire someone. I've been saying that for three years.
